I had the idea of a stop-motion style for the animation, but I had no idea how to execute it. I completed the animation with abrupt transitions, and it doesn't look natural when the character sits and stands up. I attribute this issue to a lack of references. I should have taken my own references and started the animation with them. Since I did the grease pencil animation on the character's face and around the feet by myself, I can't make significant changes in the character animation. I've made corrections where I thought necessary, but due to time constraints, I have to complete the project in its current state.

#Grease Pencil Animation

I attempted frame by frame animation with the grease pencil on the face, around the saxophone, and around the feet. I'm pleased with the result. I believe it adds a lot to the scene.

I added grease pencil details around the saxophone since I didn't include any sound.

Grease pencil adds a lot to the scene, but if I'm animating frame by frame manually, the project needs to be well-planned from the beginning. Otherwise, a sudden change can disrupt the entire animation. Grease pencil can be used very effectively. I'm considering exploring Grease pencil more in future projects.
